Project Work

Comprehensive Learning Ecosystem Transformation at Genpact

Project Context: As L&D Manager at Genpact, I led a comprehensive transformation of the learning ecosystem to align with the company's digital transformation goals and improve employee performance across multiple business units.

Challenge: The existing training programs were fragmented, lacked business alignment, and showed limited impact on performance metrics. With a workforce of over 1,000 employees across different functions, we needed a scalable, measurable learning solution.

My Role & Approach:

  • Conducted comprehensive Training Need Analysis across all business units
  • Designed integrated learning modules using ADDIE model and Gagne's 9 events
  • Implemented blended learning approach combining ILT, e-learning, and case studies
  • Established performance measurement framework using Kirkpatrick's evaluation model
  • Managed training logistics and vendor relationships for seamless delivery

Outcomes:

  • Achieved 92% training completion rate across all programs
  • Improved employee performance scores by 35% within 6 months
  • Reduced training costs by 25% through optimized resource allocation
  • Enhanced stakeholder satisfaction with training ROI visibility
  • Created a scalable learning framework adopted company-wide

Key Learnings: This project reinforced my understanding of the critical link between learning strategy and business outcomes. It also highlighted the importance of data-driven decision making in L&D, a skill that directly supports my CEO aspirations.

Innovation as a Competitive Advantage

The Enterprise Innovation course fundamentally shifted my perspective on how organizations can create sustainable competitive advantages through systematic innovation processes. This learning directly influenced my approach to L&D strategy. The innovation frameworks helped me identify untapped opportunities in talent development.

Information Security as Strategic Imperative

Course: Information Security Management

The Information Security Management course revealed how cybersecurity has evolved from a technical concern to a strategic business imperative. I learned that modern leaders must understand security risks and their impact on business continuity and reputation.

This knowledge proved crucial when designing online learning platforms and managing sensitive employee data. I implemented security-by-design principles in all L&D technology initiatives, ensuring compliance while maintaining user accessibility. The course enhanced my risk management capabilities significantly.

As I progress toward CEO-level roles, I now appreciate how security considerations must be integrated into every strategic decision. This understanding will be essential for leading organizations in an increasingly digital and threat-prone business environment.

Peer Evaluation: Leadership Presence and Communication

Peer Feedback Session

During a recent peer feedback session, my colleagues highlighted my strengths in strategic thinking and ability to connect learning initiatives to business outcomes. However, they also provided valuable feedback about my communication style during presentations.

The feedback indicated that while my content was always well-researched and valuable, I sometimes assumed too much prior knowledge from the audience. My peers suggested that I could be more effective by starting with foundational concepts and building complexity gradually.

This feedback was invaluable for my development as a future CEO. I immediately began practicing storytelling techniques and audience analysis before presentations. The result has been more engaging and impactful communication, which is essential for executive leadership. I continue to seek feedback regularly to refine my executive presence.
